# Site Actions, Why They Matter, and Value for You/Clients

1) Add “Holiday Readiness Audit” offer block  
What: Landing section with headline/subheadline, CTA to book a 30–45 min audit, and a limited-time note (until Dec 20).  
Why: Gives one clear seasonal offer; urgency increases response rates.  
Value: You get booked calls now; clients get fast wins to protect holiday revenue.

2) Add a downsell CTA (“Send me 2 questions”)  
What: Secondary CTA/form for visitors who won’t book a call.  
Why: Captures leads who are interested but time-constrained.  
Value: More conversations and list growth; clients get personal guidance without scheduling.

3) Place CTAs above the fold and after proof blocks  
What: Repeat primary CTA near hero and after testimonials/FAQ.  
Why: Reduces friction; visitors don’t hunt for the next step.  
Value: Higher conversion with same traffic; clearer path for clients.

4) Add a 3-step “How it works” strip  
What: Simple steps (Book → Show your funnel → Get 3 actions in 48h).  
Why: Removes ambiguity; sets expectations.  
Value: Fewer no-shows; clients feel guided and prepared.

5) Add a 45–60s Loom/video intro  
What: Short video explaining who you are, what happens on the call, and outcomes.  
Why: Transfers trust and conviction; people buy from people.  
Value: Warmer leads; clients feel they’re in good hands before speaking.

6) Add mini-case/proof strip near CTA  
What: 2–3 bullets of outcomes (e.g., reduced checkout drop-off, faster launch).  
Why: Proof near the decision point boosts confidence.  
Value: Higher form submits; clients see credibility instantly.

7) Add objection-killer FAQ  
What: Short answers to “Who is this for?”, “Too busy?”, “Tech changes?” near CTA.  
Why: Clears doubts that stop action.  
Value: More qualified submissions; clients know fit before booking.

8) Add a simple guarantee/commitment line  
What: “If we don’t find 2–3 quick wins, I’ll outline the next best steps anyway.”  
Why: Lowers risk perception.  
Value: More yeses; clients feel protected.

9) Add a footer mini-form (email + biggest obstacle)  
What: Always-visible form wired to Netlify Forms with notifications.  
Why: Captures intent at any scroll depth.  
Value: More leads with no extra traffic; clients get quick replies.

10) Wire all forms to Netlify Forms + email notifications  
What: `data-netlify="true"`, hidden `form-name`, honeypot; email alerts to you.  
Why: No backend needed; immediate delivery to your inbox.  
Value: Faster follow-up; clients get prompt responses.

11) Add a post-submit “reply to confirm” note  
What: Confirmation message asking them to reply with context.  
Why: Increases engagement and deliverability.  
Value: Warmer threads; clients get personal attention.

12) Add seasonal urgency line in hero/footer  
What: “Offer available until Dec 20; limited weekly slots.”  
Why: Creates a natural deadline.  
Value: Faster decisions; clients act before peak season.

13) Keep pages simple (headline, subheadline, proof, CTA)  
What: Use the “three layouts” rule: basic, with image, or with image + 3 bullets.  
Why: Fewer variables, higher conversion.  
Value: Quicker improvements; clients see a clear promise.

14) Add one qualifier field (“Biggest obstacle?”)  
What: Single textbox in forms.  
Why: Starts the convo with their words; filters low intent.  
Value: Better call prep; clients get tailored answers.

15) Add a thank-you/next-steps microcopy  
What: Show what happens after submit (email, timing, what to prepare).  
Why: Reduces anxiety and no-shows.  
Value: Higher show rates; clients arrive ready.

16) Add a light price/value anchor  
What: State advisory value (“$X value”) before showing the free/low-friction offer.  
Why: Reframes perceived value.  
Value: Better appreciation of your time; clients see the deal they get.

17) Add a “who I don’t help” line  
What: One sentence excluding poor fits.  
Why: Signals honesty; reduces misaligned leads.  
Value: Better fit pipeline; clients know they’re the right match.

18) Add social proof strip (logos/testimonial)  
What: Small logos or a strong quote near CTA.  
Why: Social proof lowers perceived risk.  
Value: Higher trust; clients feel safer engaging.

19) Add “Ask Steven” micro-widget  
What: Tiny form or mailto link inviting direct questions.  
Why: Personal touch; some prefer a direct note over forms.  
Value: Captures shy but interested visitors; clients get a human reply.

20) Track source in form submissions  
What: Hidden field with page path/offer tag.  
Why: Know which page/offer converts.  
Value: Double down on what works; clients see you optimize based on data.

21) Add an exit-intent downsell bar  
What: On exit, offer the email-only “2 ideas” downsell.  
Why: Saves abandoning visitors.  
Value: More leads; clients still get value without booking.

22) Add a sticky “Book the audit” button on mobile  
What: Floating CTA at bottom on mobile screens.  
Why: Reduces friction for phone users.  
Value: Higher mobile conversion; clients can act immediately.

23) Add a resources/download block  
What: One checklist/PDF gated by email.  
Why: Gives value upfront; captures leads not ready to talk.  
Value: List growth; clients get a useful asset instantly.

24) Add language-consistent CTAs (FR/EN)  
What: Mirror offers/CTAs in both locales.  
Why: Consistency prevents drop-off on language switch.  
Value: Better UX; clients feel served in their language.

25) Add a simple performance note  
What: Mention fast load, self-hosted assets, and privacy-friendly tracking.  
Why: Signals professionalism and respect for privacy.  
Value: Builds trust; clients expect a smooth, respectful experience.
